Common questions

Everything you've wondered about clean beauty

We answer the questions your dermatologist never had time for.

What does "clean beauty" actually mean? +
Clean beauty refers to products formulated without ingredients considered harmful or controversial — such as parabens, sulfates, synthetic fragrances, and certain preservatives. It's as much about what's left out as what's included.
Is natural always better for skin? +
Not necessarily. Both natural and synthetic ingredients can be effective or irritating depending on the individual. The key is understanding what each ingredient does and how your skin responds to it.
How do I build a simple skincare routine? +
Start with three basics: a gentle cleanser, a moisturiser, and SPF (for daytime). Once your skin is stable and comfortable, you can layer in targeted treatments like serums or exfoliants.
Can diet really affect my skin? +
Yes — significantly. Research links high glycaemic diets, dairy, and processed foods to increased breakouts in some people. Conversely, antioxidant-rich foods, omega-3s, and good hydration consistently support clearer, healthier skin.
