Why it matters

Your body is 60% water — every drop counts

Most people assume they're drinking enough water. But true hydration isn't just about quantity — it's about cellular absorption, mineral balance, and the quality of what you drink.

When you're chronically under-hydrated, your body compensates silently for years before symptoms become obvious. Fatigue, brain fog, dull skin, and digestive sluggishness are all early signals.

Know the signs

Is your body asking for more?

Dehydration rarely announces itself loudly. These subtle signs are your body's quiet requests for more water.

🧠

Brain fog & fatigue

Even mild dehydration reduces blood flow to the brain, causing difficulty concentrating, memory lapses, and afternoon energy crashes.

✨

Dull, dry skin

Skin is one of the last organs to receive water. If your complexion looks lifeless or feels tight, dehydration is often a root cause.

🌀

Digestive sluggishness

Water is essential for producing digestive enzymes and keeping things moving. Low intake leads to bloating, constipation, and discomfort.

💛

Dark urine & headaches

Your kidneys concentrate urine when water is scarce. Pale yellow is your goal — dark yellow or headaches are clear dehydration signals.

The Glow approach

Three layers of deep hydration

True inner care goes beyond sipping water. Here's how to hydrate at a cellular level.

01

Water quality first

Filtered, mineral-rich water is absorbed far more effectively than tap water. Consider adding a pinch of Himalayan salt or trace minerals to enhance cellular uptake.

02

Electrolyte balance

Sodium, potassium, and magnesium carry water into your cells. Without them, water passes straight through. Coconut water, leafy greens, and quality salt support this balance.

03

Food-based hydration

Cucumber, celery, watermelon, and leafy greens are up to 95% water — and they carry minerals alongside. Eating your water is one of the most effective strategies.

Daily practice

Simple habits that transform hydration

01

Start with 500ml on waking

You wake up dehydrated after 7–8 hours without water. Drink before coffee, before food, before anything.

02

Add lemon & minerals

Fresh lemon juice activates digestive enzymes and a pinch of sea salt replenishes electrolytes lost overnight.

03

Hydrate before you eat

Drinking 250ml 30 minutes before meals aids digestion and prevents mistaking thirst for hunger.

04

Track your colour

Pale straw-yellow urine means you're well hydrated. Use it as your daily benchmark — no app required.
